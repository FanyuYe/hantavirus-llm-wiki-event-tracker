# Hondius Hantavirus Daily Report 2026-05-13

At the 2026-05-12 briefing, WHO reported 11 cases linked to MV Hondius, including 3 deaths; 9 of the 11 were confirmed Andes virus infections and 2 remained probable. WHO said all cases were among passengers or crew, there had been no deaths since 2026-05-02, and there was no sign at that point of a larger outbreak. This replaces yesterday's caution that the French, U.S., and Spanish post-repatriation positives lacked a captured WHO/ECDC reconciliation. ## New Developments Since Last Report

- WHO updated the official reconciled count to 11 cases, 9 confirmed and 2 probable, including 3 deaths. This is the most important source upgrade today because it absorbs at least part of the post-repatriation country-level positives into a global WHO frame. [WHO DG briefing](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-dg-briefing-2026-05-12.md)
- WHO confirmed the passenger evacuation phase had completed: all passengers had disembarked and left Tenerife, and MV Hondius was on its way to the Netherlands. [WHO DG briefing](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-dg-briefing-2026-05-12.md)
- WHO set the monitoring clock for those onboard during disembarkation: last exposure was 2026-05-10, and 42-day monitoring runs to 2026-06-21. [WHO DG briefing](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-dg-briefing-2026-05-12.md)
- UKHSA published a detailed UK follow-up plan. British nationals arrived at Manchester on 2026-05-10 by dedicated flight, moved to Arrowe Park Hospital for assessment/testing, and will isolate for 45 days from last exposure, with PCR tests using blood and throat swabs. [UKHSA blog](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-ukhsa-blog-2026-05-12.md)
- WA Health said returning Australians and one New Zealander were expected to arrive at RAAF Pearce late on 2026-05-12 and then move to Bullsbrook National Resilience Centre. It said none of the passengers being repatriated to Australia were displaying symptoms. [WA Health](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-wa-health-repatriation-2026-05-11.md)
- Reporting with Reuters contribution said France had identified and was monitoring 22 contact cases, with no evidence of virus circulation in France. The same report said Dutch authorities reported all 26 passengers on the first Netherlands evacuation flight tested negative. [Guardian/Reuters](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-guardian-who-more-cases-2026-05-12.md)
- The same reporting said Radboudumc quarantined 12 staff members for six weeks after blood and urine from a hantavirus patient were handled without the required stricter protocols. This is not evidence of community spread, but it is a concrete hospital-infection-control issue to track. [Guardian/Reuters](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-guardian-who-more-cases-2026-05-12.md)

## Situation Assessment

The event has moved from a ship-disembarkation problem into a long monitoring tail across destination countries. WHO's latest count means the case baseline is no longer stuck at the 2026-05-08 DON600 number, but the public record still needs country-level mapping: WHO did not identify which new confirmed cases correspond to the French, U.S., Spanish, or other national reports.

The risk framing is stable. WHO continues to say global risk is low and there is no current sign of a larger outbreak, while warning that more cases may appear because Andes virus has a long incubation period. UKHSA's public explainer reinforces that the risk to the general public is very low and that Andes virus does not spread through everyday social contact. [WHO DG briefing](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-dg-briefing-2026-05-12.md) [UKHSA blog](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-ukhsa-blog-2026-05-12.md)

The main practical risk now is fragmentation of information. Different countries are using different isolation periods and public reporting cadence: WHO says 42 days from 2026-05-10, the UK describes 45 days from last exposure, and Australia is using Bullsbrook quarantine with further advice pending from the Australian Health Protection Committee. This is not necessarily a contradiction, but it means the timeline should preserve each country policy as stated rather than normalize them into one rule.
