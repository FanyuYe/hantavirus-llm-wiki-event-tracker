# Hondius Hantavirus Daily Report 2026-05-16

## Executive Summary

The main development since the 2026-05-15 report is stability, not escalation. ECDC's 2026-05-15 dashboard keeps the count at 11 total cases, including 8 confirmed, 2 probable, 1 inconclusive, and 3 deaths, with no new cases or deaths since the previous update. WHO's 2026-05-15 briefing phrases the current burden as 10 confirmed and probable cases with 3 deaths; that appears to exclude the inconclusive U.S. case rather than introduce a new reduction. [ECDC 2026-05-15](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-ecdc-dashboard-2026-05-15.md) [WHO briefing 2026-05-15](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-dg-briefing-2026-05-15.md)

The response has moved deeper into distributed monitoring. WHO says affected travellers are isolated and closely monitored in six countries. ECDC added technical guidance for laboratory testing and infection prevention/control, reinforcing that the active work is national surveillance, isolation, symptom-triggered care, and healthcare precautions rather than evidence of spread into the general population. [WHO briefing 2026-05-15](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-dg-briefing-2026-05-15.md) [ECDC lab advice](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-ecdc-lab-testing-advice-2026-05-14.md) [ECDC IPC advice](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-ecdc-ipc-advice-2026-05-15.md)

Country follow-up is filling in. AP reports that five Australians and one New Zealander arrived in Australia for quarantine expected to last at least three weeks at Bullsbrook, with no symptoms and no community risk reported. A UK Parliament written statement records the UK reception/testing pathway for 20 British nationals plus two non-British passengers and continued UKHSA work with public-health teams in UK Overseas Territories. ## Situation Assessment

The outbreak remains in the monitoring tail. The most important accounting point is the denominator: ECDC still tracks 11 total cases including the inconclusive case; WHO's briefing gives 10 confirmed/probable cases. For the timeline, the safest representation is "10 confirmed/probable plus 1 inconclusive" until the inconclusive U.S. case is retested or formally removed. [WHO briefing 2026-05-15](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-dg-briefing-2026-05-15.md) [ECDC 2026-05-15](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-ecdc-dashboard-2026-05-15.md)

The public-risk picture remains stable. WHO says the wider-population risk is low, and ECDC continues to assess EU/EEA general-population risk as very low. The visible response is intensive follow-up of exposed people across countries, not evidence of uncontrolled community transmission. [WHO briefing 2026-05-15](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-dg-briefing-2026-05-15.md) [ECDC 2026-05-15](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-ecdc-dashboard-2026-05-15.md)
