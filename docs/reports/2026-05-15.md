# Hondius Hantavirus Daily Report 2026-05-15

## Executive Summary

The main development since the 2026-05-14 report is a classification correction, not a larger outbreak. WHO DON601 and ECDC's 2026-05-14 dashboard both keep the global total at 11 cases and 3 deaths, but the current best public classification is now 8 laboratory-confirmed Andes virus infections, 2 probable cases, and 1 inconclusive U.S. case under further testing. This replaces the earlier shorthand of 9 confirmed and 2 probable used after WHO's 2026-05-12 briefing. [WHO DON601](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-don601-2026-05-13.md) [ECDC 2026-05-14](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-ecdc-dashboard-2026-05-14.md)

The U.S. branch also became clearer. CDC's 2026-05-13 transcript says the U.S. inconclusive case had discordant foreign laboratory results, with retesting underway in the United States. CDC recommends testing only symptomatic people, says the U.S. monitoring period is 42 days with 2026-05-11 as day one, and says no state or federal quarantine orders had been issued. AP separately reported that Dr. Stephen Kornfeld was cleared from the Nebraska biocontainment unit to join the other Americans in the National Quarantine Unit, with two other Americans being monitored at Emory. [CDC transcript](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-cdc-transcript-2026-05-13.md) [AP U.S. follow-up](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-ap-us-inconclusive-cleared-nebraska-2026-05-13.md)

The European response record now better explains the coordination behind Tenerife evacuation and repatriation. The European Commission says the Health Security Committee coordinated disembarkation, repatriation, sequencing, suspected-case reporting, quarantine/testing, and surveillance; an EU Health Task Force expert deployed onboard; and EU civil-protection mechanisms supported evacuation flights through Tenerife. [European Commission HSC](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-european-commission-hsc-2026-05-13.md)

## Situation Assessment

The outbreak remains in the monitoring tail. The most important correction is that the global total did not rise, but the classification changed: the U.S. passenger should be tracked as inconclusive, not confirmed. This matters because country-level follow-up can otherwise double-count the WHO/ECDC total. [WHO DON601](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-don601-2026-05-13.md) [CDC transcript](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-cdc-transcript-2026-05-13.md)

The public-risk assessment remains stable. WHO assesses risk as moderate for people who were onboard and low globally; ECDC assesses risk to the EU/EEA general population as very low; CDC says risk to the U.S. general public is low or extremely low in its current public pages and transcript. The response posture is intensive monitoring of exposed people, not evidence of uncontrolled community spread. [WHO DON601](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-don601-2026-05-13.md) [ECDC 2026-05-14](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-ecdc-dashboard-2026-05-14.md) [CDC transcript](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-cdc-transcript-2026-05-13.md)

Rotterdam remains the next operational checkpoint. ECDC says the ship is scheduled to arrive in the Netherlands on 2026-05-17 or 2026-05-18, while Oceanwide's latest captured operator update expected 2026-05-18 and described staggered crew disembarkation, Dutch/RIVM quarantine procedures, and ship cleaning/disinfection. [ECDC 2026-05-14](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-ecdc-dashboard-2026-05-14.md) [Oceanwide 2026-05-13](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-oceanwide-update-2026-05-13-1815.md)
