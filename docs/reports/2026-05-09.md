## New Developments Since Last Report

- ECDC's event page was still tracking 7 cases and 3 deaths, with EU/EEA public risk assessed as very low, even after page metadata showed a 2026-05-08 update. This preserves the public discrepancy with WHO's 8-case/5-confirmed baseline. [ECDC dashboard](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-ecdc-dashboard-2026-05-08.md)
- UKHSA's official baseline says British nationals still onboard could be repatriated once the ship docked if they remained asymptomatic; UKHSA was also monitoring two independent UK returnees and close contacts, with the public risk assessed as very low. [UKHSA](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-ukhsa-update-2026-05-06-captured-2026-05-09.md)
- Same-day reporting citing UKHSA says a third British national was a suspected case on Tristan da Cunha, while two British cases were already confirmed among patients in the Netherlands and South Africa. British passengers and crew were expected to isolate for 45 days after return to the UK. [Guardian UK](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-guardian-third-briton-2026-05-08.md)
- Singapore identified two residents who had been onboard MV Hondius and on the St Helena-to-Johannesburg flight with a confirmed case. Both were isolated at NCID for testing; if negative, the plan was 30 days quarantine plus surveillance to 45 days from last exposure. [Singapore CDA](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-singapore-cda-2026-05-07.md)
- St Helena moved to weekday daily public updates, continued contact-tracing assessments, reported reduced low-risk-contact numbers as data was processed, and said returning St Helena residents from the relevant Airlink flight had been identified and given advice/support. [St Helena](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-st-helena-update-2026-05-07.md)
- Tenerife reception planning shifted from only "arrival expected" to a tightly constrained evacuation operation: the ship was expected to remain at anchor near Granadilla, passengers would be evaluated onboard and transferred without local-population contact, and the operation might need completion within a narrow weather window around 2026-05-10. [Guardian Tenerife](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-guardian-tenerife-operation-2026-05-08.md)

## Situation Assessment

The event remains serious but still appears bounded by close-contact exposure and active public-health control. The most important live risk is not general-community spread; it is delayed recognition of additional cases among people who left the vessel, flew with a case, or had close/prolonged contact with cases during the incubation window. [WHO response](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-response-2026-05-07.md) [UKHSA](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-ukhsa-update-2026-05-06-captured-2026-05-09.md) [Singapore CDA](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-singapore-cda-2026-05-07.md)

For the next 24-48 hours, the operational bottleneck is Tenerife. If the ship arrives around 2026-05-10 as expected, the key question is whether Spain can complete screening, transfer, repatriation, and Spanish-national quarantine before weather disrupts ship-to-shore movement. [Guardian Tenerife](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-guardian-tenerife-operation-2026-05-08.md)
