# Hondius Hantavirus Daily Report 2026-05-10

## Executive Summary

The most important new development since the 2026-05-09 report is case-count reconciliation. UKHSA now says WHO confirmed late on 2026-05-08 that the cluster stood at 8 cases, 6 confirmed and 2 suspected, with 1 previously suspected case discounted by testing. This is newer than the WHO/Europe public page captured for 2026-05-08, which still states 8 cases, 5 confirmed and 3 deaths, and newer than ECDC's public dashboard count of 7 cases and 3 deaths. [UKHSA 2026-05-09](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-ukhsa-update-2026-05-09.md) [WHO/Europe 2026-05-08](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-europe-response-2026-05-08.md) [ECDC dashboard](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-ecdc-dashboard-2026-05-08.md)

The second major development is that national repatriation plans are now concrete. The UK plan routes asymptomatic British passengers and crew through Tenerife screening to Arrowe Park Hospital for assessment/testing and isolation planning. The U.S. plan routes American passengers by medical repatriation flight to Offutt Air Force Base and then to the University of Nebraska National Quarantine Center for exposure risk assessment and monitoring recommendations. Both governments continue to assess public risk as very low or extremely low. ## New Developments Since Last Report

- UKHSA's 2026-05-09 update reports the latest captured official case status as 8 total cases, 6 confirmed and 2 suspected, after 1 suspected case was discounted. Three British nationals are included: 2 confirmed cases hospitalized in South Africa and the Netherlands, plus 1 suspected case being monitored on Tristan da Cunha. [UKHSA 2026-05-09](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-ukhsa-update-2026-05-09.md)
- UKHSA says none of the British nationals still aboard MV Hondius were reporting symptoms, and that the ship was expected in Tenerife on Sunday 2026-05-10. British passengers and crew without symptoms are planned for escorted transfer to a chartered flight, then Arrowe Park Hospital on the Wirral for clinical assessments and testing; isolation may last up to 45 days. [UKHSA 2026-05-09](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-ukhsa-update-2026-05-09.md)
- CDC published its official U.S. response: American passengers are planned for evacuation to Offutt Air Force Base near Omaha and transfer to the National Quarantine Center at the University of Nebraska, with CDC teams deployed both to the Canary Islands and Offutt. CDC says the risk to the American public remains extremely low. [CDC](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-cdc-update-2026-05-08.md)
- Tenerife operational reporting now describes a staged, aircraft-linked transfer: the ship was expected near Granadilla early on 2026-05-10, passengers would leave by Zodiac boats, then move directly to Tenerife South Airport in Spanish army vehicles, with transfer timing constrained by a Sunday-to-Monday weather window. This remains a secondary-source operational account until an official post-arrival confirmation is captured. [Le Monde](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-lemonde-tenerife-operation-2026-05-09.md) [Guardian UK return plan](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-guardian-uk-return-plan-2026-05-09.md)
- WHO/Europe's 2026-05-08 public page preserves the low-risk global framing: Andes virus can transmit between humans only in limited circumstances involving close and prolonged contact, and WHO warns more cases may appear because of the incubation period. [WHO/Europe 2026-05-08](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-europe-response-2026-05-08.md)

## Situation Assessment

The event has moved from discovery/evacuation of acute cases into controlled international repatriation and follow-up. The risk picture is still concentrated among passengers, crew, close contacts, and people who disembarked before the outbreak was recognized, not the general public. The strongest evidence for that framing remains consistent across WHO, ECDC, UKHSA, and CDC, even while the public case counts have not fully reconciled across pages. [WHO/Europe 2026-05-08](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-europe-response-2026-05-08.md) [UKHSA 2026-05-09](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-ukhsa-update-2026-05-09.md) [CDC](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-cdc-update-2026-05-08.md)

The operational risk for the next report is timing. If the Granadilla transfer did not complete before weather worsened, some passengers or crew could remain aboard longer than planned. If it did complete, the monitoring burden shifts to national systems: UK isolation, U.S. assessment and onward monitoring, and continued tracing in places such as St Helena, Tristan da Cunha, South Africa-linked flights, Singapore, and other countries whose nationals disembarked earlier.
