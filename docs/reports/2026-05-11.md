# Hondius Hantavirus Daily Report 2026-05-11

The main development since the 2026-05-10 report is that WHO's detailed Disease Outbreak News update now directly confirms the case-count reconciliation that was previously only captured via UKHSA. ## New Developments Since Last Report

- WHO DON600 gives the detailed case list: 6 confirmed Andes virus infections, 2 probable cases, 3 deaths, 4 patients hospitalised as of 2026-05-08, and 1 suspected case ruled out after negative testing in Germany. [WHO DON600](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-don600-2026-05-08.md)
- WHO now states a working hypothesis: case 1 most probably acquired infection before boarding through environmental exposure during activities in Argentina and Chile, while current evidence points to subsequent human-to-human transmission onboard. Sequencing and epidemiological investigation remain open. [WHO DON600](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-don600-2026-05-08.md)
- WHO's risk framing is more precise than the earlier media page: cruise-ship-related risk is moderate, while global public-health risk remains low. High-risk contacts should be actively monitored and quarantined at home or in a facility for 42 days after last exposure; low-risk contacts should passively self-monitor. [WHO DON600](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-don600-2026-05-08.md)
- WHO Director-General Tedros directly addressed Tenerife on 2026-05-09, saying this was not another COVID-style event, the risk to Tenerife residents was low, no symptomatic passengers were onboard, and the Granadilla operation was selected under International Health Regulations because Tenerife had the nearest adequate capacity. [WHO DG Tenerife message](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-dg-tenerife-message-2026-05-09.md)
- Oceanwide's late 2026-05-09 operator update confirmed the planned Granadilla arrival at 05:30 local time on 2026-05-10, managed disembarkation from about 08:00, small-boat passenger transfer only in controlled groups, luggage retained onboard, and a later Rotterdam voyage after bunkering and supplies. [Oceanwide 2026-05-09](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-oceanwide-update-2026-05-09-2300.md)
- Guardian and elDiario.es reporting show the planned Tenerife operation moved into execution on 2026-05-10: Spanish passengers were moved first and reached Madrid/Gomez Ulla quarantine; UK passengers and crew were flown toward Arrowe Park quarantine; other flights covered the Netherlands, Germany, Belgium, Greece, Canada, Turkey, France, Ireland, the US, and later Australia. [Guardian evacuation](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-guardian-evacuation-2026-05-10.md) [elDiario live](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-eldiario-live-2026-05-10.md)
- Spain's Ministry of Health RSS confirms the official Spanish posture: quarantine and active surveillance for people disembarked and followed in Spain, earlier transfer of Spanish residents to Gomez Ulla, and continued very-low-general-population-risk messaging. [Spain Sanidad RSS](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-spain-sanidad-rss-2026-05-10.md)
- elDiario.es reported a possible new watch item: one French repatriated passenger developed symptoms compatible with hantavirus during the flight from the Canary Islands, and the five French passengers were isolated pending testing. Treat this as a media-reported suspected development until confirmed or ruled out by French/WHO sources. [elDiario live](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-eldiario-live-2026-05-10.md)

## Situation Assessment

The event has crossed from shipboard containment into distributed national follow-up. The case baseline is now clearer because WHO DON600 directly aligns with the UKHSA update: 8 total cases, 6 confirmed and 2 probable, with 3 deaths. The more important uncertainty has shifted from "how many cases are there?" to "will any monitored passenger, crew member, or earlier disembarked contact develop symptoms during the incubation window?"

The public-risk assessment is still reassuring for people outside the exposed group. WHO's moderate-risk language applies to the ship and exposed passengers/crew, not the global public. The operational burden is now fragmented across national quarantine and monitoring systems, especially Spain, the UK, the US, the Netherlands, France, and countries tracing earlier disembarkations from Saint Helena and Tristan da Cunha.
