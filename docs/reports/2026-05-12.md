## New Developments Since Last Report

- WHO published contact-management guidance specific to the MV Hondius Andes virus event. It treats all passengers and crew who were on board as high-risk contacts, uses disembarkation as the last-exposure date for those individuals, and recommends daily public-health follow-up plus designated-facility or home quarantine for 42 days. [WHO contact management](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-contact-management-2026-05-10.md)
- WHO also published a disembarkation/onward-management technical note covering screening, symptomatic isolation, PPE during transfers, mental-health support, luggage handling, crew restrictions, ship disinfection/rodent inspection, and risk communication. [WHO disembarkation note](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-disembarkation-technical-note-2026-05-10.md)
- UNMC/Nebraska Medicine reported that 16 U.S. citizens arrived in Omaha early Monday, 2026-05-11. One passenger tested positive but had no symptoms and was admitted to the Nebraska Biocontainment Unit; 15 others entered the National Quarantine Unit for assessment and monitoring. [UNMC U.S. arrival](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-unmc-us-arrival-2026-05-11.md)
- Reuters, citing French Health Minister Stephanie Rist, reported that one of five French MV Hondius passengers tested positive and that her condition was deteriorating. The four other French passengers tested negative but were to be retested, and French authorities had traced 22 contacts. [France Reuters](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-france-positive-reuters-2026-05-11.md)
- Guardian reporting added that the French positive case had reported symptoms onboard and that WHO later described her condition as very critical. This is a serious clinical watch item, but the critical-condition detail should be cross-checked against official WHO or French updates when available. [Guardian French/evacuation complete](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-guardian-french-critical-evacuation-complete-2026-05-11.md)
- Spanish media citing health/government sources reported one asymptomatic provisional positive PCR result among the 14 Spanish arrivals at Hospital Gomez Ulla. The other 13 tested negative, and the provisional-positive passenger awaited a second PCR. Treat this as pending confirmation. [Spain provisional positive](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-spain-provisional-positive-2026-05-11.md)
- El Pais reported an initial public sequencing result from a Swiss patient sample: Andes virus was confirmed, no reassortment or mutation indicating a new strain was found, and the sequence clustered closely with Argentina 2018-2019 Andes isolates. Broader transmission-chain interpretation still depends on additional case sequencing. [El Pais sequencing](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-elpais-genetic-sequencing-2026-05-11.md)

## Situation Assessment

The event is now less about ship reception and more about distributed national case finding. The exposure group has been dispersed into quarantine systems in multiple countries, which means the next meaningful updates are likely to arrive as country-level PCR results, clinical deteriorations or recoveries, and eventually a WHO/ECDC reconciliation.

The source-strength hierarchy matters. WHO remains the baseline for global case definitions and risk framing. UNMC/Nebraska Medicine is an official source for the U.S. arrival and quarantine status. Reuters ministerial reporting is strong for France, while Guardian adds operational and clinical color. Spain's provisional positive is important but explicitly not final.

The first sequencing report is reassuring on one narrow point: currently reported sequencing does not suggest a novel reassorted or unusually divergent Andes strain. It does not yet settle how many onboard transmissions occurred, because that requires comparing sequences from multiple patients alongside the epidemiological timeline.
