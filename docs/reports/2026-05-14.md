# Hondius Hantavirus Daily Report 2026-05-14

## Executive Summary

The main development since the 2026-05-13 report is that the outbreak has moved decisively into the monitoring tail. WHO's 2026-05-13 remarks did not revise the 11-case count from the prior WHO briefing, but they confirmed the operational status: all passengers have been repatriated, the ship is sailing to the Netherlands with crew aboard, the captain reported no symptoms onboard that morning, and affected countries should continue active monitoring and IHR reporting until the quarantine period ends on 2026-06-21. [WHO PBAC remarks](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-pbac-remarks-2026-05-13.md) [WHO DG briefing](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-dg-briefing-2026-05-12.md)

The second development is that the Rotterdam endpoint is clearer. Oceanwide's 2026-05-13 update says 27 people remain onboard, including 25 crew and 2 RIVM-provided medical staff, none displaying hantavirus-associated symptoms. The operator now expects MV Hondius to arrive in Rotterdam on 2026-05-18, followed by staggered crew disembarkation, quarantine under Dutch/RIVM procedures, and cleaning/disinfection coordinated with GGD Rotterdam, RIVM, and local authorities. [Oceanwide 2026-05-13](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-oceanwide-update-2026-05-13-1815.md)

The third development is source-quality improvement on the science signal. ECDC now provides an official statement that sequencing strongly suggests confirmed tested passenger samples share the same original infection source; the virus resembles known South American Andes viruses, is not a new variant, and has no evidence of increased transmissibility or severity. This strengthens yesterday's sequencing interpretation, which was mainly based on media/science reporting. [ECDC genomics](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-ecdc-frontline-genomics-2026-05-11.md)

- France's follow-up update says the French positive passenger remains in serious condition in ICU at Bichat Hospital. The other four French MV Hondius passengers remain negative and asymptomatic; four child contacts tested negative; and 18 other contacts are in strict quarantine without symptoms. [France follow-up](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-france-followup-2026-05-13.md)

## Situation Assessment

The case-count baseline is stable today. The best current global count remains WHO's 2026-05-12 figure of 11 total cases, 9 confirmed and 2 probable, including 3 deaths. Today's sources mostly clarify follow-up status rather than add new global cases. That means the right tracking posture is country-by-country monitoring without double-counting Spain, France, U.S., Dutch, or UK facts against the WHO total. [WHO DG briefing](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-dg-briefing-2026-05-12.md)

The risk framing is also stable. WHO continues to treat this as a serious but contained event requiring active monitoring through the full incubation window. ECDC's genomics statement reduces concern that the event is being driven by a novel or unusually transmissible variant, while still preserving the need for precaution because Andes virus can rarely spread through close and prolonged human contact. [WHO PBAC remarks](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-pbac-remarks-2026-05-13.md) [ECDC genomics](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-ecdc-frontline-genomics-2026-05-11.md)

The next practical checkpoint is Rotterdam. Until the ship arrives, crew disembarkation, crew quarantine arrangements, and the exact disinfection protocol remain planned rather than completed. Oceanwide's update is operationally useful, but final public-health clearance should come from Dutch/RIVM/GGD Rotterdam sources if they publish it. [Oceanwide 2026-05-13](../sources/hondius-hantavirus-oceanwide-update-2026-05-13-1815.md)
