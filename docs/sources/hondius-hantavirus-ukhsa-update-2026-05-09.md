## Key Claims Used

- The UK government said it was working with international authorities ahead of the return of British nationals from Tenerife to the UK after the WHO-confirmed MV Hondius outbreak.
- UKHSA stated that WHO had confirmed late on 2026-05-08 that there were 8 cases: 6 confirmed and 2 suspected, after 1 previously suspected case was discounted by testing.
- Three British nationals were included in the 8 cases: 2 confirmed cases in hospital, one in South Africa and one in the Netherlands, and 1 suspected case on Tristan da Cunha.
- The third British national case had disembarked on Tristan da Cunha because they live there and was being supported and monitored by local health services.
- None of the British nationals still aboard MV Hondius were reporting symptoms.
- The ship was expected to dock in Tenerife on Sunday 2026-05-10, with UK government staff on the ground.
- Further checks were planned for all passengers and crew before disembarkation.
- British passengers and ship crew without symptoms were to be escorted to an airport and flown back to the UK on a chartered flight under infection-prevention controls.
- On arrival in the UK, passengers were to be transferred to an isolation facility at Arrowe Park Hospital on the Wirral for clinical assessments and testing, with a 72-hour assessment window to decide whether home isolation or another location was suitable.
- British passengers and crew would be asked to isolate for up to 45 days, with UKHSA support, monitoring, and testing as required.
