## Summary

European Centre for Disease Prevention and Control source card supporting the Hondius hantavirus timeline and daily report.

## Key Claims Used

- ECDC said MV Hondius arrived at Granadilla, Tenerife, on 2026-05-10.
- ECDC said passengers and crew were disembarking and being medically evacuated to their countries of origin.
- ECDC said, at disembarkation, passengers and crew were considered high-risk and repatriated through non-commercial flights whether symptomatic or not.
- ECDC said symptomatic passengers and crew required immediate medical isolation, testing, and medical care.
- ECDC said asymptomatic people were asked to quarantine and monitor symptoms for up to six weeks.
- ECDC confirmed one new case in France: a former passenger who developed acute symptoms on the return flight to France and was in intensive care.
- ECDC said it had deployed additional experts through the EU Health Task Force on 2026-05-09 to support Spanish authorities.
- ECDC said recent genetic sequencing strongly suggested the confirmed tested passenger samples were linked to the same original source of infection.
- ECDC said genomic information showed the outbreak virus was similar to Andes viruses already known to circulate in South America and was not a new variant.
- ECDC said there was no evidence that the variant spread more easily or caused more severe disease than other Andes viruses.
