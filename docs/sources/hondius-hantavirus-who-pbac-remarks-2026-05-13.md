## Key Claims Used

- WHO Director-General Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus said he had just returned from Spain after working on disembarkation and repatriation for MV Hondius passengers and crew.
- Tedros thanked Spain, Tenerife, the European Union, South Africa, the United Kingdom, Cabo Verde, the Netherlands, and the ship's Polish captain for roles in the response.
- Tedros said the ship was sailing to the Netherlands with the crew.
- Tedros reported a same-day message from Captain Jan Dobrogowski saying there were no symptoms onboard so far.
- Tedros said all passengers had now been repatriated and would be monitored until quarantine ends on 2026-06-21.
- WHO's advice remained active monitoring in a specified quarantine facility or at home for 42 days from last exposure, which was 2026-05-10.
- WHO said symptomatic people should be isolated and treated immediately.
- WHO asked affected countries to report regularly through the International Health Regulations on the health and well-being of passengers and crew.
