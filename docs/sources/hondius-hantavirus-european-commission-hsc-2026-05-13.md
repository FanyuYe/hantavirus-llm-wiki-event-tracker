## Summary

European Commission source card supporting the Hondius hantavirus timeline and daily report.

## Key Claims Used

- The European Commission says the Health Security Committee met regularly after the 2026-05-02 EWRS notification to coordinate safe disembarkation/repatriation, sequencing, reporting of suspected cases, follow-up quarantine/testing, surveillance, and rapid information exchange.
- The Commission states that ECDC issued a Threat Assessment Brief and that the Health Security Committee issued a 2026-05-11 opinion on management and response to the Andes hantavirus outbreak.
- The Commission says an EU Health Task Force expert was deployed onboard at Cape Verde on 2026-05-06 to support epidemiological investigation and safe repatriation.
- The EU Reference Laboratory for emerging, rodent-borne, and zoonotic viral pathogens was engaged to support national reference laboratories for Andes virus diagnosis.
- The Commission says latest sequencing strongly suggests cases stem from the same very recent zoonotic spillover event and that there is no indication of increased transmissibility or severity compared with other Andes viruses, while further investigation remains needed.
- Through the Union Civil Protection Mechanism, the Commission supported Spain and coordinated evacuation and transport support with several Member States and participating states; France, the Netherlands, Greece, and Ireland conducted EU-coordinated and co-funded evacuation operations, with Tenerife South Airport as the operational hub.
- The Commission says a Norwegian-hosted rescEU medical evacuation aircraft was mobilised and pre-positioned in Tenerife, and additional rescEU transport/logistics capacities and protective equipment remained on standby.
