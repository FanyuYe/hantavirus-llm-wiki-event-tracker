## Key Claims Used

- WHO frames the note for public-health, port-health, and partner agencies involved in MV Hondius disembarkation, onward travel, monitoring, and follow-up.
- The note sets a controlled-disembarkation structure with health screening, clinical management of symptomatic people, infection-prevention precautions, risk communication, luggage handling, crew management, and ship sanitation.
- For symptomatic passengers or crew, WHO recommends isolation in a designated area with dedicated bathroom and personal items while transfer or evacuation is organized, plus PPE for health workers and respirator use for the patient during transfer.
- For asymptomatic passengers after disembarkation, WHO points back to the contact-management guidance and recommends daily follow-up for 42 days after disembarkation, avoidance of contact with others in a designated facility or home, and prompt reporting/testing if symptoms appear.
- WHO says crew members should not resume duty on another ship until active monitoring and quarantine are complete.
- WHO advises ship inspection for rodents, cleaning, disinfection, and rodent-control measures as appropriate before the vessel is no longer regarded as affected by the competent authority.
- The risk-communication annex says authorities should explain that disembarkation and onward travel are being conducted through coordinated controlled public-health procedures and that wider-public risk remains low, while still emphasizing that exposed people may later develop symptoms.
