## Key Claims Used

- WHO Director-General Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus said WHO was first informed of the MV Hondius hantavirus cluster 10 days before the 2026-05-12 briefing.
- WHO asked Spain to accept the vessel after assessing that Cabo Verde did not have sufficient capacity to manage full disembarkation and repatriation.
- Spain, WHO, the Netherlands, and the ship operator coordinated the disembarkation and repatriation operation over the two days before the briefing.
- Tedros said all passengers had disembarked and left Tenerife, and MV Hondius was on its way to the Netherlands.
- WHO's updated event count at the briefing was 11 cases, including 3 deaths, all among passengers or crew on the ship.
- WHO said 9 of the 11 cases were confirmed Andes virus infections and the other 2 were probable cases.
- WHO said there had been no deaths since 2026-05-02, when WHO was first informed of the case cluster.
- WHO assessed that there was no sign of a larger outbreak at the time of the briefing, while warning that more cases could appear in coming weeks because of the long incubation period.
- WHO recommended active monitoring in a specified quarantine facility or at home for 42 days from last exposure, which WHO identified as 2026-05-10 for the disembarkation group, ending on 2026-06-21.
- WHO requested regular country reporting on passengers and crew through the International Health Regulations channel.
