# Public health measures activated for two Singapore residents onboard MV Hondius

## Summary

Singapore Communicable Diseases Agency source card supporting the Hondius hantavirus timeline and daily report.

## Key Claims Used

- Both individuals were isolated at the National Centre for Infectious Diseases and were being tested for hantavirus.
- One had a runny nose but was otherwise well; the other was asymptomatic.
- CDA assessed risk to the Singapore general public as low.
- The two individuals were onboard MV Hondius when it departed Ushuaia, Argentina, on 2026-04-01.
- One was a 67-year-old Singaporean male who arrived in Singapore on 2026-05-02.
- The other was a 65-year-old Singapore permanent resident male who arrived in Singapore on 2026-05-06.
- Both had disembarked from the ship and were also on the same flight as a confirmed hantavirus case from St Helena to Johannesburg on 2026-04-25.
- CDA said that confirmed case did not travel to Singapore and had since died in South Africa.
- If the two Singapore residents tested negative, CDA planned 30 days of quarantine from the date of last exposure, repeat testing before release, and phone surveillance for the remaining monitoring period up to 45 days from last exposure.
- If either tested positive, the person would remain hospitalized for monitoring and treatment, and contact tracing would be conducted.
