## Key Claims Used

- Guardian reported that dozens of passengers and crew were evacuated from MV Hondius in a two-day operation that began on Sunday 2026-05-10 in Tenerife.
- British passengers and crew were among those removed from the vessel and flown to the UK for hospital quarantine in Merseyside; the report said a plane carrying 22 UK citizens landed in Manchester at about 21:00 Sunday.
- Spanish passengers were screened, taken off first, moved by coach to Tenerife airport, and flown onward; the Spanish government confirmed 14 Spanish citizens landed at Madrid airport on Sunday evening.
- The ship arrived in the Canary Islands in the early hours of Sunday carrying 146 people. No one else onboard had symptoms, according to the report.
- Flights were arranged for passengers from the UK, Netherlands, Germany, Belgium, Greece, Canada, Turkey, France, Ireland, and the US. A later flight to Australia with 6 people was expected Monday afternoon.
- Guardian reported that the 19 passengers and 3 crew from the UK were to quarantine at Arrowe Park Hospital in Wirral, then self-isolate for 45 days at home after welfare checks.
- Guardian reported that 24 Filipino stewards/hotel staff were being transferred to the Netherlands for quarantine and that 14 Filipino deck/engine staff would remain onboard as essential crew for the voyage to Rotterdam.
- Guardian reported the vessel was anchored slightly offshore of Granadilla and that passengers were taken to the dock in groups of 5 to 10 only when aircraft were ready.
- The report said PCR diagnostic testing was not being carried out on the ship; passengers had temperature checks and completed a health survey, while receiving countries could test after departure.
- The report said WHO and Spanish authorities stated passengers and crew would not contact people in Tenerife.
