## Key Claims Used

- UKHSA said it was monitoring and providing public-health advice about the MV Hondius-linked hantavirus outbreak.
- UKHSA reported that 3 foreign nationals had died in connection with the outbreak, while the risk to the UK public remained very low because hantavirus is not spread through everyday social contact.
- UKHSA said the type of virus in the outbreak had been confirmed as Andes hantavirus by the National Institute for Communicable Diseases in South Africa and Geneva University Hospitals in Switzerland.
- British nationals arrived from Tenerife into Manchester airport on 2026-05-10 on a dedicated flight, using strict infection-control measures during the journey.
- The passengers were transferred by private coach to an isolation facility at Arrowe Park Hospital on the Wirral for clinical assessment and testing.
- UKHSA said passengers would isolate for 45 days from last exposure, with regular testing and NHS/UKHSA care.
- UKHSA said PCR testing was being performed on blood samples and throat swabs, including for asymptomatic passengers, with results expected within 24 hours of each sample.
- UKHSA said anyone testing positive or developing symptoms would be transferred from Arrowe Park to a specialist infectious diseases centre.
- UKHSA health protection teams planned daily supportive checks by phone or text for the full monitoring period after passengers left Arrowe Park.
- UKHSA said Andes virus had never been seen in the UK rodent population, and that no large-scale public-health intervention such as lockdown was warranted.
