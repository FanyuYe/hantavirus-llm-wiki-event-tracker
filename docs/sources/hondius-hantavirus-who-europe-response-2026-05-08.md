World Health Organization source card supporting the Hondius hantavirus timeline and daily report.

## Key Claims Used

- Dr Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus briefed media on the cluster linked to MV Hondius.
- WHO/Europe stated that 8 cases had been reported so far, including 3 deaths.
- WHO/Europe stated that 5 of the 8 cases had been confirmed as hantavirus.
- The virus involved was identified as Andes virus, described as the only species known to be capable of limited human-to-human transmission linked to close and prolonged contact.
- WHO assessed the public-health risk as low while noting that more cases could be reported because of the incubation period.
- WHO said it was coordinating closely with multiple countries under the International Health Regulations.
