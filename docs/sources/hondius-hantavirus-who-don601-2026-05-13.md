## Key Claims Used

- WHO published the third Disease Outbreak News report for the MV Hondius-linked hantavirus cluster.
- As of 2026-05-13, WHO reported 11 total cases, including 3 deaths, with a case fatality ratio of 27%.
- WHO classified the 11 cases as 8 laboratory-confirmed Andes virus infections, 2 probable cases, and 1 inconclusive case undergoing further testing.
- Since WHO DON600 on 2026-05-08, WHO said two additional confirmed cases were reported from France and Spain, and one inconclusive case was reported from the United States.
- The French case became symptomatic during repatriation; the Spanish case tested positive after arrival and was well/asymptomatic at the time described by WHO; the U.S. case was asymptomatic and had discordant laboratory results from two laboratories.
- WHO states all laboratory-confirmed cases were confirmed as Andes virus infection and all cases were passengers onboard MV Hondius.
- WHO preserves the working hypothesis that the first case acquired infection before boarding through land exposure, with current evidence suggesting subsequent human-to-human transmission onboard.
- WHO says preliminary sequencing shows close, near-identical sequences from different cases.
- Contact tracing and monitoring continue for passengers who disembarked at Saint Helena on 2026-04-24, Praia/Cabo Verde on 2026-05-06, and Tenerife/Spain on 2026-05-10 and 2026-05-11, plus flight contacts with possible exposure to subsequently confirmed cases.
- WHO assesses risk as moderate for people who were onboard the cruise ship and low at the global level.
