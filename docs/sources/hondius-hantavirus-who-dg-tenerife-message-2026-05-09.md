## Key Claims Used

- WHO Director-General Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus addressed the people of Tenerife directly ahead of the MV Hondius reception operation.
- He stated that the event was not another COVID-style event and that WHO assessed the current public-health risk from hantavirus as low.
- He described the virus involved as the Andes strain of hantavirus, noted 3 deaths, and said there were no symptomatic passengers on board at the time of the message.
- He said a WHO expert was on the ship and medical supplies were in place.
- The described Spain/WHO plan was for passengers to be ferried ashore at the industrial port of Granadilla, kept away from residential areas, moved in sealed guarded vehicles through a cordoned-off corridor, and repatriated directly to their home countries.
- He said WHO's request to Spain was made under the International Health Regulations and that Tenerife was chosen because it was the nearest port with sufficient medical capacity, infrastructure, and operational ability.
- Tedros said he intended to travel to Tenerife to observe the operation firsthand.
