## Key Claims Used

- EFE reported that the French passenger infected with Andes hantavirus remained in serious condition in intensive care at Bichat Hospital in Paris.
- Four school-age children considered contact cases had tested negative.
- The other four French MV Hondius passengers also remained negative and had no symptoms, according to the French government communication cited by EFE.
- Eighteen other contacts were in strict quarantine and under rigorous medical care, and none was symptomatic.
- The report said the 22 French contacts were linked to a Dutch woman who had been an MV Hondius passenger and died from hantavirus on 2026-04-26 in Johannesburg after flying there from Saint Helena.
- The French government said it was using a meningitis-like close-contact protocol to define close contacts of a hantavirus-positive person.
- Under that approach, a close contact involved proximity within 2 metres for more than 15 minutes in the 10 days before the positive test.
- France presented this procedure to European partners seeking harmonisation at a high health-security standard.
