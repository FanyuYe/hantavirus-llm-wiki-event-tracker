## Key Claims Used

- MV Hondius was expected to approach the industrial Port of Granadilla between 04:00 and 06:00 local time on Sunday 2026-05-10.
- The operation was described as weather-dependent and designed to avoid the ship touching the Canary Islands coast.
- About 80 passengers from 23 countries and 17 crew members were to be evacuated by Zodiac boats, then transferred directly to Tenerife South Airport in Spanish army vehicles.
- The Spanish interior minister reportedly said nationals from each country would disembark in stages only when the repatriation plane for that country was ready.
- Thirty crew members were expected to remain aboard to sail the ship to the Netherlands for decontamination.
- Countries including France, Germany, Belgium, Ireland, and the Netherlands were reported to be sending repatriation planes; two EU RescEU civil-protection planes were to assist countries unable to charter aircraft.
- Authorities said the operation needed to be completed between Sunday noon and Monday evening because worsening sea conditions from Tuesday could make transfers impossible.
- The report framed local concerns as political and procedural as well as health-related, while noting official assurances of no civilian contact.
