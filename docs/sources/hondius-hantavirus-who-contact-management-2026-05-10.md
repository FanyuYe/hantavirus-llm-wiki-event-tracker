## Key Claims Used

- WHO advises a risk-based approach to identifying, monitoring, and managing contacts of probable or confirmed Andes virus cases from the MV Hondius event.
- WHO reiterates that Andes virus has documented but limited human-to-human transmission, usually linked to close and prolonged contact.
- WHO's operational definitions classify suspected, probable, confirmed, and non-case status; non-cases who later develop compatible symptoms during the maximum incubation period should be retested and reclassified if needed.
- For this event, WHO defines the date of last exposure for MV Hondius passengers and crew as the date of disembarkation.
- WHO considers all passengers and crew who were on board MV Hondius to be high-risk contacts under a precautionary approach.
- High-risk contacts should receive daily public-health follow-up for 42 days after last known exposure and remain in a designated facility or home quarantine depending on national policy and capacity.
- High-risk contacts are advised to avoid unnecessary national and international travel for 42 days; any compatible symptoms should trigger prompt isolation, clinical evaluation, and testing.
- Low-risk contacts should self-monitor daily for 42 days after last exposure; symptoms should be promptly reported and evaluated.
