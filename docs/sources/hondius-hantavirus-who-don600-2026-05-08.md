## Key Claims Used

- WHO reported that, as of 2026-05-08, the MV Hondius-linked cluster stood at 8 cases, including 3 deaths; 6 cases were laboratory-confirmed hantavirus infections, all identified as Andes virus.
- WHO classified the 8 cases as 6 confirmed and 2 probable, including 3 deaths: 2 confirmed and 1 probable. The case fatality ratio reported for the event was 38%.
- WHO said 147 passengers and crew were onboard when the event was notified, and 34 passengers and crew had previously disembarked.
- WHO reported 4 patients hospitalised as of 2026-05-08: 1 in intensive care in Johannesburg, South Africa, 2 in different hospitals in the Netherlands, and 1 in Zurich, Switzerland.
- One previously suspected case transferred to Germany tested negative by PCR and serology and was no longer considered a case.
- The ship left Cabo Verde on 2026-05-06 for the Canary Islands, Spain, where disembarkation was planned.
- WHO's working hypothesis was that case 1 most probably acquired infection before boarding through environmental exposure during activities in Argentina and Chile, with current evidence pointing to subsequent human-to-human transmission onboard. Sequencing and epidemiological investigations remained ongoing.
- WHO assessed the public health risk related to the cruise ship as moderate, and the global risk as low.
- WHO advised monitoring early symptoms for 42 days after last potential exposure and recommended active monitoring plus home or facility quarantine for high-risk contacts for 42 days after last exposure.
