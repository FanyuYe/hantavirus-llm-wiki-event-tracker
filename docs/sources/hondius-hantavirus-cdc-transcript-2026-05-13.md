## Summary

U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention source card supporting the Hondius hantavirus timeline and daily report.

## Key Claims Used

- CDC said the risk to the general U.S. public remained low.
- CDC said it had more than 100 staff working on the response and had deployed teams to the Canary Islands and Nebraska.
- CDC said two CDC team members joined the American passenger repatriation flight back to Nebraska, and another CDC team met the plane on arrival.
- CDC said it recommends testing only for people with symptoms; CDC also said it is working with states and partners on other testing and research modalities.
- CDC said the monitoring period is 42 days and that 2026-05-11 was day one for people departing the ship.
- CDC described the U.S. inconclusive case as having one positive and one negative result abroad, with retesting underway in the United States.
- CDC said no state or federal quarantine orders had been issued; it was working with contacts, passengers, state health departments, and local health departments to support monitoring.
- CDC clarified that shared-flight exposure reports involved people who had left the ship before the outbreak was detected, while passengers still on the ship after detection were transported to Nebraska on a private flight.
