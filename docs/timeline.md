# Hondius Hantavirus Outbreak Timeline 2026

## Current Snapshot

As of the latest verified official sources captured on 2026-05-08, the outbreak is linked to the Dutch-flagged expedition vessel _m/v Hondius_ after a South Atlantic itinerary that departed Ushuaia, Argentina, on 2026-04-01. WHO's 2026-05-07 media note reports 8 total cases, including 5 laboratory-confirmed hantavirus cases and 3 deaths. WHO identifies the virus as Andes virus, the hantavirus species known to be capable of limited person-to-person transmission after close and prolonged contact. WHO continues to assess the public-health risk as low while warning that more cases could be reported because of the incubation period. [WHO response](sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-response-2026-05-07.md)

The core unresolved question is how exposure occurred: environmental exposure during South America or remote-island travel remains possible, and limited person-to-person transmission is being considered because Andes virus is involved. [WHO DON](sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-don599-2026-05-07.md) [ECDC assessment](sources/hondius-hantavirus-ecdc-assessment-2026-05-06.md)

## Standing Interpretation

The incident is not a routine cruise-ship gastrointestinal outbreak. It is a rare severe respiratory illness cluster caused by hantavirus, now identified by WHO and ECDC as Andes virus. The epidemiological concern is narrower than COVID-style respiratory spread but higher than most hantavirus clusters because Andes virus can rarely transmit between people through close and prolonged contact. [WHO response](sources/hondius-hantavirus-who-response-2026-05-07.md) [ECDC response](sources/hondius-hantavirus-ecdc-response-activated-2026-05-06.md)

The main control strategy visible in the official record is coordinated case care, evacuation of symptomatic or high-risk contacts, onboard medical monitoring, laboratory testing, contact tracing across countries, and controlled arrival/disembarkation in the Canary Islands.
